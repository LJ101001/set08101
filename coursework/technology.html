Technology We Use

Sales CRM

Customer relationship management (CRM) software is a wildly popular tool for managing customer data from a single point of entry. Its popularity is hardly accidental. As an all-in-one sales platform solution, most leading CRM software contains most, if not all, of the technology types on this list. That’s because businesses use CRM to track sales, store contact details, analyze performance, run forecasts, and automate dozens of tasks that are necessary for maintaining healthy customer relationships.

Sales tracking software

Sales tracking technologies facilitate the tedious data entry of sales reporting by managing all aspects of the sales process. The software provides set workflows so reps can instantly pinpoint where any prospect is in the buyer’s journey, as well as decide on what the next steps should be towards conversion. Many of these apps are also armed with sales analytics tools to help identify potential areas of improvement.

Email analytics

Use email analytics to track how recipients on your contact list respond to and interact with your campaigns. By tracking metrics like open, clickthrough, and unsubscribe rates, you can see what makes your prospects engage or jump ship and use this information to refine your messaging.Making the most of email analytics can be a straightforward way to get more leads moving into and through your sales pipeline.

Sales forecasting software

With so much data on hand, reports are necessary to make sense of it all. Reporting software synthesizes CRM data into usable sales insights with easy-to-interpret visuals. As a sales management tool, reports are crucial for sales leaders to understand performance and coach individual reps in the areas that will most benefit them. By utilizing reporting software, managers no longer need to painstakingly compile data and build spreadsheets.
